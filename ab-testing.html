An introduction to A/B Testing
### What is A/B Testing?
Using statistics to confirm a positive bias towards one of two prestented versions of content with a single variation.
Using data to tell us how to convert better.
  • Deliberately simple.
  • Focused on a single change.
  • Does not have to be a small change.
  • Isolated to a single test.
### What _is not_ A/B Testing?
  • Making changes based off feelings.
  • Voting on changes.
  • Testing more than one change on the same page at a time.
### A/B Testing is _not_ about A vs B
#### The goal is to use results to predict future impacts.
#### We're using the visitors included in the tests, to help determine what will make future visitors convert.
#### Those statistics can take a long time.

### If you make assumptions about your target audience, you’ll eventually get it wrong.
### 95th, 50th, median? ![Confidence Interval](images/ab-testing/confidence-example.png)
### What is a null hypothesis? Automatically assume that your variation is NOT a meaningful improvement. We either: - Disprove it by the variation winning - Or fail to disprove it
### “innocent until proven guilty” A defendant’s innocence is legally supposed to be the underlying assumption unless proven otherwise.
### Your control is the best Assume your control will out-preform your variation, until your variation achieves statistical significance
### Statistical _what_?
### Statistical Significance
### Imagine running an A/A test You would expect that, as with any test sample, you'd have variation between two groups with the same results.
Essentially, the significance level is a value we set based on the level of accuracy we deem acceptable. The industry standard significance level is 5%, which means we are seeking results with 95% accuracy.

### So should _you_ test?

#### ...but not really _all_ the things.
#### Focus on the following
#### High Traffic Pages To acheive a statistical winner, you need lots of traffic.
#### Test "Up Funnel" Compare traffic on your funnel steps before you start making tests on your last step of the funnel.
#### Test Shared Elements Elements shared on many pages will get lots of sessions, and can help you find easy wins.
#### Use Heat Maps Services like Crazy Egg and Hot Jar can give you information on where people interact with your content the most.
