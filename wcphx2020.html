What do engaged employees prefer in their managers?

  • Feel they can talk about nonwork-related issues.
  • Can approach their manager with any type of question.
  • Helps them set work priorities.
  • Helps set performance goals.
  • Focuses on strengths and positive characteristics.

What can we do to achieve these things?

Learn what drives each individual.

  • Money
  • Challenges
  • Family time
  • Travel
  • Public Visibility
  • Mentouring

Schedule and keep frequent one-on-one meetings with each employee.

  • Put them on a recurring schedule
  • Never miss a schedule
  • Encourge them to come with an agenda
  • Leave time between mettings to prepare
  • Allow for general conversation about life
  • Don't use it as a status meeting

Create a professional development plan based off their goals.

  • Attend Conferences
  • Courses
  • Certifications
  • Learn a new skill
  • Encourage and ask about progress
  • Be ready to assist and enable them

Assign tasks to strenghts when possible

Start with taking care of yourself

You cannot drink from an empty cup

  • Schedule focus times on your calendar
  • Make your own professional goals
  • Read books to fill in your talent gaps
  • Know your limits
Podcasts, meeting blocks, focus blocks

What is your new 'productive'?

How do you measure your productivity without contributing?

  • Measure your teams productivity
  • Identify areas blocking your team and clear them
  • Maintaining culture and morale

Things I've found helpful.

Using Calendly to have meetings scheduled

It is aware of my calendar, and gives the employee the ability to schedule at their best times.

Find your tribe

Find other people who have made, or are making this same transition, and meet with them. Mastermind style.

Be honest and transparent

You don't have to be the person who always has it togather.

Communicate Asynchronously

Real-time chat tools tend to generate a sense of urgency and panic. Tools like Basecamp/Trello/GitHub allow for more thoughtout and complete discussions.

Some things to avoid.

Don't ask "Do you have a minute to chat?"

Always define why you want to chat. Alwasy try and give a time, adquate warning, and an agenda.

Don't Assume your own preferences are shared

Just because that is how you liked something while you were a contributor, doesn't mean the new primary contributor will prefer it that way.

Never be the first to share your opinion no a discussion.

As managers, our voice carries a lot of weight. By speaking out first on what we prefer, we can cause a bias in the discussions.
