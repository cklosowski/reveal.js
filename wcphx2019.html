## Work/Home Separation
![Meme of what my friends, clients, governmnet, mom, myself think I do, and what I really do](images/wcphx2019/working-from-home-meme-5.jpg)
![The Great Divide, ASU and U of A football helmets pointing at eachother](images/wcphx2019/divide.jpg)
Short-Term side affects of poor work/life balance at home and at work. Burnout is REAL. 30 hours of great work is better than 40 hours of mediocre work. Missing important life events, doesn't have to be the norm.
Are you working more than 55 horus a week? Answering an email from the couch, is work. Checking slack channels, is work. 1 Minute to grab my phone, check my missed emails and slack messages (that needed no actions from me) Does it need to be now, can it wait until morning.

66% of full-time U.S. Employees strongly believe they do not have work/life balance.

Stats and images from Small Biz Trends infograph
  • Be realistic…
  • Be Flexible…
  • Adjust your workspace…
  • Plan Ahead…
  • Designate an "office"…
  • Discuss it…
Planning ahead can include 'scheduling' the time you need to make up. No surprises, sets expectations, and leaves your mental space clear for when that will be done.
